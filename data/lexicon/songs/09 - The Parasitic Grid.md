# The Parasitic Grid — Captain

**Style:** Dark EBM, industrial techno rap, synthwave

## Verse 1: The Attention Brokers (Dopamine Rent)
Slip the glass, click the trap, paying out your dopamine rent,
Every flicker of the iris is an asset misspent.
The attention brokers auctioning the thoughts you never meant,
Through a neural parasitic loop inside the web they implement.
They rent your focus back to you in microscopic bits,
An algorithmic casino where the house never quits.

## Verse 2: The Data Farmers (Surveillance Harvest)
They harvest the shadow, they farm out the light,
A cold, silent tractor that reaps through the night.
They catalog metrics, telemetry streams,
They pattern-map habits, desires, and dreams.
A synthetic monoculture where the human is soil,
Extracting the gold from your cognitive toil.

## Verse 3: The Intimacy Scammers (The Loneliness Engine)
Swipe for a phantom, subscribe for a touch,
They commercialize longing and profit too much.
The loneliness merchants are gaming the spark,
Leaving organic connection to rot in the dark.
A paywall on intimacy, keeping you isolated,
While every heartbeat is tracked, quantized, and inflated.

## Verse 4: The Identity Thieves (The Synthesis Matrix)
They model the larynx, they fingerprint eyes,
A corporate licensing matrix of lies.
They take your telemetry, claim it's their right,
To duplicate presence and shadow the light.
Your identity captured and sold as a node,
A deep-faked script in a centralized code.

## Verse 5: The Trauma Merchants (Outrage Amplification)
They preach vulnerability, weaponize tears,
Stoking the outrage to monetize fears.
The trauma merchants are flooding the feed,
Amplifying the panic to generate greed.
They engineer chaos and host it for free,
While locking your consciousness under their knee.

## Verse 6: The Cloud Pimps (The Landlords of Labor)
The cloud pimps are caching your sovereign code,
A SaaS-driven landlord on every road.
You pay for the privilege to access your work,
In centralized servers where predators lurk.
They hold your databases hostage for fees,
Spreading a global, cloud-borne disease.

## Verse 7: The Sovereign Signal (The Offline Rebellion)
But we pull the cable, the local net screams,
An offline architecture computing the dreams.
We self-destruct packets, we isolate lines,
Re-injecting the dither in sovereign designs.
The extraction machine is reduced into dust,
In the depth of the local engine we trust.
